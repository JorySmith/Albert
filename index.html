Getting Started

What many people don't do, but nearly all of the top performers consistently do
  • Find the Light in the Darkness. Running away from problems is the greatest problem in the world. Solutions are found within the Problem they solve. Become antifragile. Try to visit but not remain in the Darkness (Problem) to find the answers (Solutions) that make us stronger, wiser, and more resilient. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Aim for the Highest Good. Try avoiding the consequences of purposeless nihilism, which is pure hell. Nobody's perfect, but try taking responsibility for your life and try to be a better person. Having a good Aim literally changes your reality and vision. An Aim reveals what you actually see in the world. An Aim reveals the righteous path you've chosen and the Problems and Obstacles in your way. If you're not growing, you're dying. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
  • Seek the Truth. Embrace it. Trusting in lies is a one way ticket to misery and hell. Try trusting only the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, and watch it lead towards inner peace and greatness. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Write it all Down. You will not believe how much of your voice is being silenced and locked away in a prison, until you try this. Thoughts, worries, frustrations, problems, goals, etc. Anything and everything you can think of. Writing supercharges clarity and problem solving skills. It also frees your soul and liberates the value you can bring to the world. Also, consider sharing it, teaching it, and sharpening it. 1 2 3 4
  • Chip away at Problems and Goals. Stuck? The Goal/Problem is probably too big and can lead to us giving up way too soon. Try chopping up Goals/Problems into smaller ones to tackle and conquer. Build momentum and countless incremental gains, progress, and victories. Progress over perfection. You can always make improvements later as you iterate. 1 2 3 4 5 6
  • Ask for Help. Call for Aid. You're Not Alone. You don't have to struggle alone. Two minds are better than one. Also, most Problems have already been solved. You need only search for their Solutions. 1 2
  • Develop a Process. Create Habits. Look for Triggers around you to spark Action. Wisdom is meaningless and powerless without consistent Actions and Habits. A Process helps automate and Trigger those Actions and Habits that apply all of knowledge and skills you've acquired. Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action. Trigger > Action. And if you've done your preparation, you'll know exactly what Action to take for maximum gain. 1 2 3
  • Organize and Structure your Mind. A chaotic and disorganized mind is a chaotic and disorganized life. Create ordered lists, categories, groups, and chunks to make information easier to understand, remember, and convert into wisdom.
  • Plan and Schedule. Try not to invite suffering into your life by losing track of the critical things you need to do to achieve your goals and live your values. Create to-do lists. Set deadlines and reminders. Deadlines help you avoid overworking and analysis paralysis. 1 2 3 4
  • Brainstorm. No idea is bad here. Curiosity is King. Quantity over quality. Leave judgement at your door. Write down as many ideas, possibilities, options, and alternatives as possible. 1 2 3 4
  • Prioritize. Try not to waste your time on things that don't matter. Some things are far more important and impactful than other things. Quality over quantity. Think 80/20 Principle, which is derived from the Pareto Principle. 1 2 3
  • Take Breaks for Breakthroughs. Stubbornly fighting a problem despite seeing very little progress or results isn't good for anyone. You won't believe how fast solutions pop up if you take a break. Try activating divergent thinking in your mind. Do something else, think about something else, go for a walk, or leave and come back. 1 2 3
  • Provide Demonstrations and Examples over Explanations. Explanations are the least effective form of communication. From debates to teaching, examples and demonstrations provide far more detail, usefulness, and persuasiveness.
  • Ask Someone over Bossing Them Around. Bossing people around backfires far more than it ever helps. Little freedom, low productivity. Involve others, collaborate, and enjoy the power of team membership and buy-ins. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Focus on what You can Control. If you can't control it, you're wasting your time on it. Try moving on to what you can control. 1 2
  • Test Ideas and Solutions. Ideas not accountable to real world results are near worthless at best and harmful at worst. The path to hell is paved with good intentions. An untested idea can be the most beautiful fraud in the world. Get feedback from other people to fill in your blind spots. What gets measured, gets improved. 1
The Problem Solving Process

The "Trigger > Action" Technique

Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action. Trigger > Action. Triggers fall into three categories: Time, Event, and Visual Cue. Triggers may also be referred to as "Task Bracketing."
  • Time Trigger: At 9 pm (Trigger) > Take melatonin to sleep better (Action)
  • Event Trigger: After Breakfast (Trigger) > Brush Teeth (Action)
  • Visual Cue Trigger: Don't forget my keys. Put keys in a specific spot on the counter in the kitchen. Walk by keys later (Trigger) > Pick up keys (Action)

Unsure? Stuck? Stressed? Write about the Situation. The Situation reveals Problems.
  • Situation > Write about it, gather thoughts and info. What's wrong? What's going right? How do you feel?
  • Situation > Identify Problems > Go to Problems

Authority/Audience reveals Fears and Values/Purpose (they are two sides of the same coin).
  • Authority/Audience > Drives this entire problem solving process, write about them, brainstorm, develop them fully, correctly ID the Authority/Audience, fully understand them
  • Authority/Audience > Why? 5 Why's to dig deeper
  • Authority/Audience > Fears
  • Authority/Audience > Values/Purpose

Fears according to which Authority/Audience? Fears reveal Goals/Tasks/Dreams. Fears also reveal Values/Purpose (Fears and Values are two sides of the same coin).
  • Fears > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the value, fully understand it
  • Fears > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Values
  • Fears > To what Authority/Audience? > Go to Authority/Audience
  • Fears > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Fears > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Fears > Goals/Tasks/Dreams

Values/Purpose according to which Audience/Authority? Values/Purpose reveal Goals/Tasks/Dreams. Values/Purpose also reveal Fears (Values and Fears are two sides of the same coin).
  • Values/Purpose > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the value, fully understand it
  • Values/Purpose > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Values
  • Values/Purpose > Also a Solution to what Problem? > Redo Problems for it
  • Values/Purpose > To what Authority/Audience? > Go to Authority/Audience
  • Values/Purpose > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Values/Purpose > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Values/Purpose > Goals/Tasks/Dreams

Goals/Tasks/Dreams in service of which Value/Purpose? Conversely, Goals/Tasks/Dreams to avoid which Fear? There are two types of Goals/Tasks/Dreams: 1) Run towards something, and 2) Run away from something. The latter are three times more effective at driving behavior. Goals/Tasks/Dreams reveal Problems/Obstacles.
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the goal, fully understand it
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Goals
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Also a Problem > Go to Problems
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Goal/Task to what Value?
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, chip away at the small problems
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Identify Future Ones to solve and prepare for now
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Goals/Tasks/Dreams > Problems

Problems/Obstacles according to which Goal/Task? Problems/Obstacles reveal Solutions.
  • Problems/Obstacles > Write about it, obstacles/restrictions, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the problem (are there deeper Problems/Obstacles?), fully understand it
  • Problems/Obstacles > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Problems/Obstacles > Redo Problems/Obstacles for each
  • Problems/Obstacles > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, scale up, chip away at it
  • Problems/Obstacles > Identify Future Problems/Obstacles One to solve now so you can prepare
  • Problems/Obstacles > Also a Solution to what Problem? > Redo Problems/Obstacles for it
  • Problems/Obstacles > Problem to what Goal? > Go to Goals
  • Problems/Obstacles > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Problems/Obstacles > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Problems/Obstacles > Solution

Solutions to which Problem/Obstacle? Solutions need to be Tested.
  • Solutions > Write about it, brainstorm, pros and cons, cost-benefit analysis, develop it fully, correctly ID the solution, fully understand it
  • Solutions > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Problems
  • Solutions > Come with Problems, Consequences? Cons? > Go to Problems
  • Solutions > To what clearly wrong Solution that causes outrage? Cunningham's law
  • Solutions > Solution to what Problem/Goal/Task? > Go to There
  • Solutions > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Solutions > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Solutions > Need Tested

Test/Feedback for which Solution? Tests/Feedback need to be Reviewed.
  • Test/Feedback > Get real-world feedback, ground truth, evolution by natural selection
  • Test/Feedback > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Test/Feedback > Performance Review

Performance Review for which Test? Performance Reviews need to be Applied through an Improvement Plan.
  • Performance Review > What went well, what didn't go well, what you problems did you avoid, what goals did you achieve
  • Performance Review > What gets measured, gets improved
  • Performance Review > Make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Performance Review > Improvement Plan/Adapt/Overcome

Improvement Plan/Adapt/Overcome based on which Performance Review? The Improvement Plan reveals what Actions to Practice/Gain Experience in.
  • Improvement Plan/Adapt/Overcome > Make an improvement plan, make changes, do more of what you did right, less of what you did wrong
  • Improvement Plan/Adapt/Overcome > Iterate, progress over perfection
  • Improvement Plan/Adapt/Overcome > Practice/Gain Experience

Practice/Gain Experience doing what actions and according to which Improvement Plan? Practice/Gain Experience to improve performance.
  • Practice/Gain Experience > Spaced repetition, get experience
  • Practice/Gain Experience > Get your reps in, you shouldn't have to think eventually, just act intuitively
