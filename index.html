Getting Started

What many people don't do, but nearly all of the top performers consistently do
  • The light is found in the darkness. The solutions are found within the problem. We must visit, not stay in the darkness to find the answers we seek. 1
  • Look for triggers in the world. Like an alarm clock, they let you know you need to act. And if you've done your preparation, you'll know exactly what action to take for maximum gain. 1 2
  • Trigger > Action. The shorthand this application uses.
  • Aim for the highest good. Reveal the righteous path ahead of you or suffer the terrible consequences of purposeless nihilism. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Find and embrace the real truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth. Trusting in lies is a one way ticket to misery and hell. 1 2 3
  • Write it down: Everything and often. Talk about it, teach it, and share it. You will not believe how much of your voice is being silenced and locked away in a prison, until you try this. 1 2 3
  • Small incremental gains, progress, and victories over trying to make something perfect. You can always fix it later as you iterate. 1 2 3 4 5 6
  • Get Organized and Structured: Makes everything easier to find, remember, understand, and implement.
  • Brainstorm: Quantity over quality. Write down as many options and alternatives as possible. 1 2 3 4
  • Prioritize: Quality over quantity. Some things are far more important and impactful than others. 80/20 Principle. 1 2
  • Take breaks for breakthroughs. 1 2 3
  • Demonstrations and examples over explanations. Far more helpful and useful.
  • Create a To-Do List: Only 1 thing to remember instead of countless things. 1 2 3 4
  • Schedule and Plan. 1 2
  • Ask someone over bossing them around: Involve others, collaborate, and enjoy the power of buy-ins. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Focus on what you can control: If you can't, move on to what you can. 1 2
  • Test solutions and be accountable to the real world results. 1
The Problem Solving Process

Unsure? Stuck? Stressed? Write about the Situation. The Situation reveals Problems.
  • Situation > Write about it, gather thoughts and info. What's wrong? What's going right? How do you feel?
  • Situation > Identify Problems > Go to Problems

Audience/Authority decides Values.
  • Audience/Authority > Write about it, decides everything, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the audience, fully understand it
  • Audience/Authority > Why? 5 Why's to dig deeper
  • Audience/Authority > Values

Values reveal Goals/Tasks. Values decided by which Audience/Authority?
  • Values > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the value, fully understand it
  • Values > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Values
  • Values > Also a Solution to what Problem? > Redo Problems for it
  • Values > To what Audience? > Go to Audience
  • Values > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Values > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Values > Goals/Tasks

Goals/Tasks reveal Problems. Goals/Tasks in service of which Values?
  • Goals/Tasks > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the goal, fully understand it
  • Goals/Tasks > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Goals
  • Goals/Tasks > Also a Problem > Go to Problems
  • Goals/Tasks > Goal/Task to what Value?
  • Goals/Tasks > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, chip away at the small problems
  • Goals/Tasks > Identify Future Ones to solve and prepare for now
  • Goals/Tasks > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Goals/Tasks > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Goals/Tasks > Problems

Problems reveal Solutions. Problems according to which Goals/Tasks?
  • Problems > Write about it, obstacles/restrictions, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the problem (are there deeper problems?), fully understand it
  • Problems > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Problems > Redo Problems for each
  • Problems > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, scale up, chip away at it
  • Problems > Identify Future Problems One to solve now so you can prepare
  • Problems > Also a Solution to what Problem? > Redo Problems for it
  • Problems > Problem to what Goal? > Go to Goals
  • Problems > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Problems > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Problems > Solution

Solutions need to be Tested. Solutions to which Problems?
  • Solutions > Write about it, brainstorm, pros and cons, cost-benefit analysis, develop it fully, correctly ID the solution, fully understand it
  • Solutions > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Problems
  • Solutions > Come with Problems, Consequences? Cons? > Go to Problems
  • Solutions > To what clearly wrong Solution that causes outrage? Cunningham's law
  • Solutions > Solution to what Problem/Goal/Task? > Go to There
  • Solutions > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Solutions > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Solutions > Need Tested

Test Results need to be Analyzed. Tests for which Solutions?
  • Tests > Get real-world feedback, ground truth, evolution by natural selection
  • Tests > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Tests > Test Results Analysis

Test Results Analysis needs to be Applied. Results Analysis for which Tests?
  • Test Results Analysis > What went well, what didn't go well, what you problems did you avoid, what goals did you achieve
  • Test Results Analysis > What gets measured, gets improved
  • Test Results Analysis > Make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Test Results Analysis > Adapt/Overcome

How you Adapted to and Overcame a Problem reveals what to Practice. Adapt/Overcome according to what Test Results Analysis?
  • Adapt/Overcome > Make a plan, make changes, do more of what you did right, less of what you did wrong
  • Adapt/Overcome > Iterate, progress over perfection

Practice improves performance.
  • Practice > Spaced repetition, get experience
  • Practice > Get your reps in, you shouldn't have to think eventually, just act intuitively
Why Create this Application?

Struggling at solving problems holds us back a great deal at work and at home. I feel I'm not living up to my potential and I don't think I'm alone in that feeling.

I also feel really frustrated that I can't remember all of the fundamentals, tips, and tricks I've learned over the years. There are just too many things to remember, especially when stakes are high or I'm simply just tired.

However, getting better at solving problems can make us incredibly valuable, not only at our companies and for ourselves, but especially to our families and communities. So I set out to create an online solution that anyone can use, anywhere, anytime.
