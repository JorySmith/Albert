Problem Solving from First Principles

"If I had an hour to solve a problem, I'd spend 55 minutes thinking about the problem and 5 minutes thinking about solutions."
- Albert Einstein

Running away from problems is the greatest problem in the world.
Try running toward problems and facing fears.
The solutions are found within.

Getting Started

What many people don't do, but nearly all of the top performers consistently do.
However, nobody's perfect and nobody can claim perfect adherence to these principles.
Progress over perfection.
  • 1) Find the Light in the Darkness. Running away from Problems is the greatest Problem in the world, which is understandable because it takes true courage to willingly walk toward and take on danger. However, ignoring Problems only makes them grow bigger and more dangerous with time. Solutions are found within the Problem they solve. Try becoming antifragile by learning, growing, and thriving from your Problems and Fears. Try to visit but not remain in the Darkness (Problems) to find the Answers (Solutions) you seek. Especially the scariest Problems that undoubtedly contain the Solutions that will help you grow stronger, wiser, and more resilient. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 2) Aim for the Highest Good. Everything You Do Matters. Try avoiding the consequences of purposeless nihilism, which is pure hell, and hell is a bottomless pit. In the end, when you look back on your life, how would you feel about it? Would you be able to admire and love yourself? Nobody's perfect, but try taking responsibility for your life and try to be a better person. Having a good Aim literally changes your reality and vision. An Aim reveals what you actually see in the world. An Aim reveals the righteous path you've chosen and the Problems and Obstacles in your way. If you're not growing, you're dying. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 3) Seek the Truth. Avoid Lies. Trusting in lies is a one way ticket to misery and hell. Lies will imprison you while the truth will set you free. Try telling and trusting only the Truth. That can be very difficult so try not telling and trusting Lies. Watch it lead toward inner peace and greatness. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 4) Write it all Down. Talk about it. You will not believe how much of your voice is silenced, handcuffed, and locked away in a prison. That is until you try this. Try writing for five minutes about thoughts, worries, frustrations, fears, problems, goals, etc. Anything and everything you can think of. Writing supercharges clarity, memory, and problem solving skills. It also frees your soul and liberates the value you can bring to the world. Also, consider sharing it, teaching it, and sharpening it. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 5) Chip away at Problems, Goals, and Solutions. Be Present in the Now. Stuck? Emotional? Losing hope and feel like giving up? You probably haven't written down the Problem, Goal, or Solution. It's also probably too big or takes too long to accomplish or overcome, which can lead to giving up way too early. Take a break and try breaking it down into smaller steps or shorter time frames to tackle and conquer. Build momentum and countless incremental gains, progress, and victories. Grow your victory resume. You can always make improvements later as you iterate. Progress over perfection. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 6) Ask for Help. Call for Aid. You're Not Alone. You don't have to struggle alone. Two minds are better than one. Also, most Problems have already been solved. You need only search for their Solutions or ask for help. In fact, one of the fastest ways to find the correct Solution is to share the wrong Solution publicly (or at least your guess), and watch as the community feels compelled to correct the error immediately and without hesitation, which is Cunningham's Law. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 7) Develop a Process. Create Habits. Look for Triggers around you to spark Action. Wisdom is meaningless and powerless without consistent Actions and Habits. A Process helps automate and Trigger those Actions and Habits that apply all of the knowledge and skills you've acquired. Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action. Trigger > Action. And if you've done your preparation, you'll know exactly what Action to take for maximum gain. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 8) Organize and Structure your Mind. A chaotic and disorganized mind is a chaotic and disorganized life. Try creating sorted lists, categories, groups, and chunks to make information easier to understand, remember, and convert into wisdom. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 9) Plan and Schedule. Set Deadlines. Try to avoid inviting suffering into your life by losing track of the critical things you need to do to achieve your Goals and live your Values. Try creating to-do lists and timelines as well as setting reminders and deadlines. Deadlines help you avoid overworking and analysis paralysis. Deadlines also turn infinite suffering into finite frustrations. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 10) Brainstorm Ideas. Research. Explore. Discover. Try to avoid restricting your options at the beginning of any venture because it will only limit your capabilities and degrade overall performance. Try considering many options initially to maximize the chances of choosing the best direction and most effective action. Assume Murphy's Law will prevail and that anything that can go wrong, will go wrong. Try Brainstorming future problems to solve now before they plague you and your team later. While Brainstorming, no idea is bad. Try to express your deepest Curiosity and Creativity. Leave judgment at your door. Try writing down as many ideas, possibilities, options, and alternatives as possible. Quantity over quality. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 11) Prioritize what Matters. Try to avoid wasting time on things that don't matter. Some things are far more important and impactful than other things, also known as the 80/20 Principle, which is derived from the Pareto Principle. Quality over quantity. Try narrowing your focus and attention by filtering, reducing, and prioritizing the most important things. Also, emotions and feelings can reveal what truly matters to you. Try exploring them. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 12) Take Breaks for Breakthroughs. Stubbornly fighting a problem despite seeing very little progress or results is the definition of insanity and isn't good for anyone. You won't believe how fast solutions pop up if you take a break. Try activating divergent thinking in your mind. Do something else, think about something else, go for a walk, or leave and come back. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 13) Provide Specific Examples, Stories, and Demonstrations over Explanations. Explanations are the least effective form of communication. From debates to teaching, a far better option is to provide specific Examples, Stories, or Demonstrations because they contain incredible detail, usefulness, and persuasiveness. A single picture is worth a thousand words. Try seeking mentors and role models, which are living Demonstrations of excellence in your eyes. Try letting your walking do the talking. Show, don't tell. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 14) Collaborate over Bossing People Around, Including Yourself. If you want to move slower, undermine outcomes, and degrade relationships, then bossing people around, including yourself, is a great strategy. Little freedom, low productivity. Being bossed around can really hurt, and hurt people tend to hurt people, especially themselves. Try involving others, collaborating, and enjoying the power of community, agency, and shared causes. Don't force your opinions and conclusions on other people unless you want to bask in rejection. Try giving the evidence instead of your conclusion. Try being a collaborator over a dictator. Ask, don't order. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 15) Focus on what You can Control. If you can't control it, you're wasting your time on it. The longer you focus on what you can't control, the farther away you get from your Goals and Values. Try moving on to what you can control and where you can make a difference. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 16) Test Ideas and Solutions. Try it. Experiment in the Real World. Ideas not tested in the real world and unaccountable to true feedback, have no skin in the game. Therefore, untested ideas are near worthless at best and harmful at worst. The path to hell is paved with good intentions. An untested idea can be the most beautiful fraud in the world. Try getting feedback from other people to fill in your blind spots. What gets measured, gets improved. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 17) Have Fun. Play. Laugh. A team, organization, or country devoid of Fun or Laughter is a surefire sign that a totalitarian dictatorship has taken over. When that happens, joy and meaning are crushed and replaced with misery and hopelessness, which are a deadly combination. Try finding the Fun and Laughter in what would otherwise be a boring or trivial task. Find the Light in the Darkness. Your community will be thankful you did. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 18) Have Gratitude and You'll Always have Hope. The opposite of Gratitude is ungratefulness and resentment, and resentment is one of the foundational elements of evil. Ungratefulness and resentment hide all of the opportunities standing right in front of you, regardless of the situation. Try being Grateful for your current situation. Gratefulness will enhance your vision and reveal the path toward Hope of a better today, and tomorrow. We can either say "Thank you" or "Goodbye." 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 19) Discover and Explore Your Uniqueness. Assuming a solution is a one size fits all for your problems or goals reveals a deep misunderstanding of who each of us are. We are incredibly and richly unique in every sense of the word. Yes of course, we all share a lot in common, however, we also hold within us a very unique life and distinct perspective only we can speak for. To ignore our uniqueness is to argue we have no differences, no individuality, and no soul to call our own. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
  • 20) Write Your Principle Here. Only You can Provide the Final Principle. Any effort without collaboration will always be and always remain incomplete because we are all unique and two minds are better than one. So I humbly reach out to you with a call for aid. Would you mind providing the final principle so we can build this together? Maybe we can go faster alone, but we can go much further together. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
The Problem Solving Process

The "Trigger > Action" Technique

Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action.
Trigger > Action.
Triggers fall into three categories: Time, Event, and Visual Cue.
The "Trigger > Action" Technique may also be referred to as "Task Bracketing."
  • Time Trigger:
    At 9 pm (Trigger) > Take melatonin to sleep better (Action)
  • Event Trigger:
    After Breakfast (Trigger) > Brush Teeth (Action)
  • Visual Cue Trigger:
    Don't forget my keys. Put keys in a specific spot on the counter in the kitchen. Walk by keys later (Trigger) > Pick up keys (Action)

Not sure where to start? Write about the Situation for five minutes.
The Situation can reveal anything about any step in the problem solving process.
  • The Situation > Write about what's going on, gather your thoughts and feelings about The Situation (feelings reveal what's important to you)
  • The Situation > What's going wrong? What's going right? How do you feel about it?
  • The Situation > Brainstorm ideas
  • The Situation > Identify a Value/Fear, Goal, or Problem to focus on. Go there.

Authorities drive the entire problem solving process.
Authorities can reveal the Situation, Values, Fears, Goals, and Problems.
  • Authority > Correctly identify, analyze, and understand the Authority/Audience
  • Authority > Write about them
  • Authority > Brainstorm who they might be
  • Authority > Prioritize the key decision maker(s)
  • Authority > What is the Situation?
  • Authority > Reveals Core Values and Core Fears, go there

Values and Fears are what Authorities and Leaders care most about in the world.
Values and Fears can reveal Goals.
Values and Fears are two sides of the same coin.
Identify a Value (Strength, Wisdom) and you immediately know the associated Fear (Weakness, Stupidity), and vice versa.
Identify a Value or Fear, ask, "Why this Value/Fear?" Keep asking and answering for five times and you'll typically arrive at a truly Core Value or Fear.
Probably the most important Values and Fears are the ones you're hiding and ignoring because they contain the greatest insights into your true character.
  • Value and Fear > Write about it, study it, understand it, solve it
  • Value and Fear > Brainstorm Values and Fears
  • Value and Fear > For each idea, ask, "Why this Value/Fear?" Keep asking and answering for five times, which is applying the Five Whys structured analytic technique to find deeper and more important Core Values and Fears
  • Value and Fear > Prioritize Values and Fears that matter most
  • Value and Fear > Values/Fears reveal Goals and Problems > Go there

Goals help achieve Values and avoid Fears.
Goals are also Solutions to Problems.
Goals reveal their Solutions and Problems.
There are two types of Goals: 1) Achieve/Get something of Value, 2) Avoid a Fear.
Goals to avoid Fears are 100-200% more effective at driving behavior than Goals to achieve Values.
Try combining them: Get in better shape and Avoid being fat.
Can you feel the difference? It sure isn't fun to hear but we can either take the less painful way toward our Values, or the more effective way.
Progress over perfection.
Not making progress toward at least one of our Goals significantly contributes to feelings of depression and anxiety.
We innately need to feel progress toward our Goals or our body will start to fire the alarm bells to try to get us back on track to living our Values.
It's the journey that matters most, not the destination.
  • Goals > In service of which value or avoidance of which fear?
  • Goals > Who are these Goals for? What Authority or Audience?
  • Goals > What are the Goals?
    1) Brainstorm a list of Goals. Write them down, study them, and understand them.
    2) Get organized by creating lists, categories, groups, and chunks.
  • Goals > Which Goals are the most important?
    Prioritize the list of Goals.
  • Goals > Plan and Schedule. Create To-Do Lists, set Reminders and Deadlines. Move on and relax!
  • Goals > Not sure how to answer any of these questions?
    Ask for help. Search for answers.
  • Goal > Who is this Goal for?
    The Authority or Audience.
  • Goal > What is the Goal?
    Create the Goal's' Name. Suggested format: "Achieve something and Avoid something."
  • Goal > Write about it, study it, and understand it
  • Goal > Why is this Goal important?
    What Value and Fear does this Goal serve? Not sure yet? Ask "Why is this Goal important?" and answer it five times to find the Value and Fear this Goal serves. The last answer is the Value and Fear.
  • Goal > How do you achieve this Goal? You need Solutions and Actions. Go to Solutions.
  • Goal > Plan and Schedule. Create To-Do Lists, set Reminders and Deadlines. Move on and relax!
  • Goal > Not sure how to answer any of these questions? Ask for help. Search for answers.
  • Goal > Encounter a Problem or Obstacle? Go to Problems.
  • Goal > Having trouble trying to achieve the Goal? Feel like giving up? The Goal is probably too big or takes too long to achieve, which can lead to giving up way too early. Try breaking down the Goal into smaller ones or shorter time frame Goals to tackle and conquer. Build momentum and countless incremental gains, progress, and victories. Progress over perfection.

Problems are obstacles in the way of Goals and Solutions.
Problems reveal their Solutions.
  • Problem > Correctly identify, analyze, and understand the Problem
  • Problem > Write about it
  • Problem > Brainstorm ideas
  • Problem > For each idea, ask, "Why this Problem?" Keep asking and answering for five times to find deeper and more important Problems
  • Problem > Prioritize Problems that matter most
  • Problem > Identify future Problems to solve now and prepare for later
  • Problem > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Problem > Stuck? Break it down into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Problem > Reveals Goals, go there
  • Problem > Reveals Solutions, go there

Solutions resolve Problems and achieve Goals.
Solutions need Tested in the Real World to get crucial Feedback.
  • Solution > Correctly identify, analyze, and understand the Solution
  • Solution > Write about it
  • Solution > Brainstorm ideas
  • Solution > For each idea, ask, "Why this Problem?" Keep asking and answering for five times to find deeper and more important Solutions
  • Solution > The quickest way to the best Solution/Answer is to share the Wrong Solution/Answer with a large group of people, i.e. Cunningham's Law, and watch people clamor at the opportunity to prove you wrong (and give you the best Solution/Answer as a result)
  • Solution > Prioritize Solutions that matter most
  • Solution > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Solution > Stuck? Break it down into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Solution > Needs Tested in the Real World to get crucial Feedback, go there

Real World Tests provide critical feedback for Solutions.
Real World Tests and Feedback need to be Reviewed.
  • Real World Test > Get real-world feedback, ground truth, evolution by natural selection
  • Real World Test > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Real World Test > Review of Test Results, Performance

Reviews reveal insights of Real World Tests.
Reviews need to be implemented through an Improvement Plan.
  • Review > What went well, what didn't go well, what you problems did you avoid, what goals did you achieve
  • Review > What gets measured, gets improved
  • Review > Make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Review > Improvement Plans, Adapt, Overcome

Improvement Plans apply the insights of Reviews.
The Improvement Plan reveals what Adapted Actions to Practice and Gain Experience in.
  • Improvement Plan > Make an improvement plan, make changes, do more of what you did right, less of what you did wrong
  • Improvement Plan > Iterate, progress over perfection
  • Improvement Plan > Adapted Actions, Practice, Gain Experience

Adapted Actions carry out the Improvement Plan.
Practice these Actions and Gain Experience to get better incrementally, achieve Goals, and live Values.
  • Actions > Spaced repetition, get experience
  • Actions > Get your reps in, you shouldn't have to think eventually, just act intuitively
