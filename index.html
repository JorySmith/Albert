  • 1) Find the Light in the Darkness.

    Running away from problems is the greatest problem in the world, which is understandable because it takes true courage to willingly face and take on danger. However, ignoring problems only makes them grow bigger and more dangerous with time.

    Solutions are found within the problem they solve. Try becoming antifragile by learning, growing, and thriving from your problems and fears. But be careful of focusing on obvious problems because they're probably symptoms of much deeper and more important problems that need solved. You don't have to dig very deep to find something truly tragic in anyone's life. And it's these deep problems and pains that contain much more impactful solutions and insights.

    Try visiting but not remaining in the darkness to find the answers you seek. Especially the scariest problems that undoubtedly contain some serious pain and discomfort but also the most powerful solutions that can help you grow stronger, wiser, and more resilient.

  • 2) Aim for the Highest Good. Find your Vision. Everything You Do Matters.

    Try to avoid the consequences of purposeless nihilism, which is pure hell, and hell is a bottomless pit of problems. In the end, when you look back on your life, how would you feel about it? Would you be able to admire and love yourself?

    Nobody's perfect, but try taking responsibility for your life. Try being a better person today than you were yesterday. Are you living in service of others or in selfish slavery to yourself? Who are you accountable to? Having a good aim literally changes your reality and vision. An aim reveals what you actually see in the world. An aim reveals the righteous path you've chosen and the problems and obstacles in your way.

    If you stumble and fail at an obstacle on your path, try to avoid beating yourself up for it because it'll just create five more problems and obstacles in your way. Try dusting yourself off, fixing what went wrong, getting back on the horse, and moving forward. If you're not moving forward and growing, you're dying.

  • 3) Seek the Truth. Avoid Lies.

    Telling lies and trusting lies are a one-way ticket to misery and hell. Lies may not take you to hell immediately, but the destination is certain. It's when, not if. Lies will imprison you, while the truth will set you free. Lies erode trust in relationships and trust is the foundation of all healthy, strong, and deep relationships.

    Arrogance and excessive pride precede many downfalls. Why? Because these traits generally indicate someone has become complacent and abandoned the truth. This person probably has a narcissistic conviction that he or she knows the truth above anyone else or reality itself, which is a lie. So try showing humility and gratitude because they are potent antidotes to the toxic and intoxicating feelings of arrogance and excessive pride that destroy the weak, and the strong.

    Try telling the truth and only trusting the truth. That can be very difficult, so try not telling lies and not trusting lies. Watch as it leads toward inner peace and greatness.

  • 4) Write it all Down. Talk about it.

    You will not believe how much of your voice is silenced, handcuffed, and locked away in a prison. That is until you try writing for five minutes about your thoughts, worries, frustrations, fears, problems, goals, etc. Anything and everything you can think of. Try writing about the activities where you experience the most meaning. Try writing about what you want and where you want to go, especially your core identity and values. What do you stand for? What are your greatest strengths? Your greatest strengths tend to be related to your greatest weaknesses. Just try to get your thoughts out of your mind and onto paper, or talk about them with someone you trust.

    Writing and talking about anything supercharge clarity, memory, and problem solving skills. Writing and talking bring ephemeral thoughts and abstract ideas into the real world so you can face them. Once you can face thoughts and ideas, you can understand, learn, and grow from them. Writing and talking also free your soul and liberate the value you can bring to the world. Consider sharing, teaching, and sharpening what you've discovered on your writing and talking journey.

    The foundational goal of mental health therapeutic problem solving is self-awareness through self-exploration, expression, and understanding. Self-awareness necessitates that we write and talk about what we've discovered on our self-exploration journey. Try bringing your unconscious mind into the world through writing and talking to reveal its secrets, tragedies, and treasures. You just might discover a few surprising life-changing miracles.

  • 5) Chip Away at Goals, Problems, and Solutions. Be Present in the Now.

    Feeling unmotivated, stuck, emotional, or just wanting to give up? You probably haven't clearly defined your problem or goal in writing, which can make it really difficult to understand and tackle effectively. You might be trying to do too much at a time, brute force a solution that's not working, or comparing your Chapter 2 progress to someone else's Chapter 10, all of which can be incredibly demoralizing. You also might be forgetting to reduce the friction between you and showing up to start progress, such as forgetting to lay out gym clothes the night before a gym session. All of these actions can insidiously implant the feeling that showing up and taking the first step, or just the next step, look way too daunting. So we give up.

    Remember, simply showing up is half the battle. In addition, each step you take, whether it's the first, 20th, or last, is a decisive victory and clear proof you can do this. The first step and last step are usually the hardest steps to take. But if you can create the conditions that help you show up, you are already halfway to your goal. Then all you need to do is focus on taking that next step. That next step of victory that proves you can definitely do this and nothing is going to stop you from achieving your goals and living your values.

    It's also important to remember we all suck when we start something new. We all face failure and a ton of mistakes, especially at the beginning of any effort, and especially champions because they see mistakes more clearly and feel them more intensely than most. But each step we take is a decisive victory, and we need to try to develop the habit of taking that next step to build countless victories. Try to develop the habit of taking bad options off the table for discussion, like quitting or harmful behaviors, and start focusing on the next good option to take, like one more step in the right direction. Our habits transform us into the people we are so try taking a break to better understand the problem you're trying to solve or the goal you're trying to achieve. Then try breaking it down into smaller tasks that require less energy and motivation to get started or keep going. Try setting shorter timeframe goals to help you accomplish those crucial initial victories or maintain your current victory momentum.

    Ask yourself, "What small step am I actually willing to take right now?" Identify this critical small step and then aim for it. Show up to start your journey or get back on the victory path. Watch as your small victories build momentum, grow your victory resume, and ultimately solidify your confidence and conviction. Imagine how you'll feel if you're victorious. Imagine how you'll feel if you fail to start or keep going and let down the people you care about most. You can always make improvements as you iterate. Progress over perfection.

  • 6) Ask for Help. Call for Aid. You're Not Alone.

    You don't have to struggle alone. Two minds are better than one and most problems have already been solved. You need only search for their solutions or ask for help. Asking questions and listening can become some of your most powerful problem solving tools.

    It can feel scary asking for help. It can feel like you're exposing yourself as someone who doesn't know what they're doing. However, nobody knows everything and champions rarely rely on just themselves. We also know it usually feels better to give than it is to receive. So try to avoid robbing people of their chance to help you, especially the people you lead. We all want to feel impactful and contribute to the mission. Try enabling that by asking for help when you need it.

    One of the fastest ways to find the correct solution is to share the wrong solution publicly -- or at least your guess -- and watch as the community feels compelled to correct the error immediately and without hesitation, which is Cunningham's Law.

  • 7) Collaborate over Bossing People Around, Including Yourself.

    If you want to move slower, undermine outcomes, and degrade relationships, then bossing people around, including bossing yourself around, is a great strategy. Little freedom, low productivity. Being bossed around can make us quite undisciplined and really hurt, and hurt people tend to hurt people, especially themselves.

    Try involving others, collaborating, and enjoying the power of community, agency, and shared causes. Try to avoid forcing your opinions and conclusions on other people unless you want to grow a seed of misery into a tree of resentment. Try giving the evidence instead of your conclusion. Try being a collaborator over a dictator. Ask, don't order, or at least have the respect to provide the context and reasoning to support the order. Try asking questions, listening, and reading body language to understand before talking to be understood.

  • 8) Develop a Process. Create Habits. Look for Triggers around you to spark Action.

    Wisdom is meaningless and powerless without consistent Actions and Habits. A Process helps automate and Trigger those Actions and Habits that apply all of the knowledge and skills you've acquired. Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action. Trigger > Action. And if you've done your preparation, you'll know exactly what Action to take for maximum gain.

  • 9) Organize and Structure your Mind.

    A chaotic and disorganized mind is a chaotic and disorganized life. Try creating sorted lists, categories, groups, and chunks of information to make it easier to understand, remember, and convert into wisdom.

  • 10) Plan and Schedule. Set Deadlines.

    Try to avoid inviting suffering into your life by losing track of the critical things you need to do to achieve your goals and live your values. Try creating schedules, to-do lists, to-don't lists, and timelines as well as setting reminders and deadlines. Try scheduling something to look forward to and watch it generate energy, hope, and optimism. Deadlines help you avoid overworking and analysis paralysis. Deadlines also provide a welcome finish line and turn infinite suffering into finite frustrations.

  • 11) Brainstorm Ideas. Research. Explore. Discover.

    Try to avoid restricting your options at the beginning of any venture because it will only limit your possibilities, degrade performance, and may send you down the wrong path. The opportunity cost of going down the wrong path can be quite costly. Try considering many options initially to maximize the chances of choosing the best direction and most effective action.

    While Brainstorming, no idea is bad. Leave judgment at your door because it's quite destructive to brainstorming. Try to express your deepest Curiosity and Creativity. Try writing down as many ideas, possibilities, options, and alternatives as possible. Quantity over quality.

    Assume Murphy's Law will prevail and that anything that can go wrong, will go wrong. Then try doing a premortem, i.e., brainstorming future consequences and problems to solve now before they plague you and your team later.

  • 12) Prioritize what Matters.

    Try to avoid wasting time on things that don't matter. Some things are far more important and impactful than other things, also known as the 80/20 Principle, which is derived from the Pareto Principle. Quality over quantity. Try narrowing your focus and attention on the things that matter by filtering, reducing, and setting aside the noise. Less is more. Also, emotions and feelings can reveal what truly matters to you. Try exploring them.

  • 13) Take Breaks for Breakthroughs.

    Stubbornly fighting a problem despite seeing very little progress or results is the definition of insanity and isn't good for anyone. Trying to brute force your way to progress may work in the end, but it will usually produce a worse result on top of costing you significantly more time. You won't believe how fast great solutions pop up if you simply take a break and let your subconscious mind perform its magic.

    Try activating divergent thinking in your mind. First, write down what you're struggling with. Then, do something else, think about something else, go for a walk, leave and come back, or relax and do nothing. Try letting your mind go on a journey. Your subconscious mind knows exactly what you need and where to find it. Try letting it do its job. You need only have the courage to let go for a moment.

  • 14) Provide Specific Examples, Stories, and Demonstrations over Explanations.

    Explanations are the least effective form of communication. From debates to teaching, a far better option is to provide specific examples, stories, or demonstrations because they contain incredible detail, usefulness, and persuasiveness. A single picture is worth a thousand words. Imagine the power of a demonstration.

    Try seeking mentors and role models, which are real world demonstrations of excellence in your eyes. Be careful who you choose because who you emulate or surround yourself with the most, is who you become. Also, if someone isn't providing you with a specific example, they might be trying to force their conclusion on you instead of allowing you to come to your own conclusion. Try watching people's actions. Are they practicing what they preach? Are they demonstrating the effectiveness of their words? Try letting your walking do the talking. Show, don't tell.

  • 15) Focus on what You can Control.

    If you can't control it, you're wasting your time on it. The longer you focus on what you can't control, the farther away you get from your goals and values. Try moving on to what you can control and where you can make a difference.

  • 16) Test Ideas and Solutions in the Real World. Try it. Experiment.

    Ideas not tested in the real world and unaccountable to true feedback, have no skin in the game. Therefore, untested ideas are near worthless at best and harmful at worst. The path to hell is paved with good intentions. An untested idea can be the most beautiful fraud in the world. Try prototyping, creating proof of concepts, and crafting minimum viable products (MVPs) to test in the real world and get true feedback. Try getting feedback to fill in your blind spots, especially from people that vehemently disagree with you or the idea because they'll provide the longest list of all the weaknesses. What gets measured, gets improved.

  • 17) Have Fun. Play. Laugh.

    Any person, team, organization, or country devoid of Fun and Laughter is a surefire sign that a totalitarian dictatorship has taken over. When that happens, joy and meaning are crushed and replaced with misery and hopelessness, which are a deadly combination. Try finding the Fun and Laughter in a situation, especially in boring or trivial tasks. Try laughing with people, having fun in places, playing with hobbies, and enjoying music and stories. Find the Light in the Darkness. Your community will be thankful you did.

  • 18) Have Gratitude and You'll Always Have Hope and Perspective.

    The opposite of Gratitude is ungratefulness and resentment, and resentment is one of the foundational elements of evil. Ungratefulness and resentment hide all of the opportunities and treasures standing right in front of you, regardless of the situation. No matter how bad a situation gets, we can always make it far worse by not having gratitude. Try being Grateful for your current situation. Gratefulness will enhance your vision and reveal the path toward Hope of a better today, and tomorrow. We can either say "Thank you" or "Goodbye."

  • 19) Discover and Explore Your Uniqueness and Value.

    Assuming a solution is a one-size-fits-all for your problems or goals reveals a deep misunderstanding of who each of us are. We are incredibly and richly unique in every sense of the word. Yes of course, we all share a lot in common, however, we also hold within us a very unique life and distinct perspective only we can speak for. Try exploring your uniqueness, interests, and values. Try showing some respect for yourself. To ignore our uniqueness is to argue we have no differences, no individuality, and no soul to call our own.

  • 20) Only You can Provide the Final Principle. Write Your Principle Here.

The Problem Solving Process

The "Trigger > Action" Technique

Triggers are like an alarm clock, clear reminders in the world to take Action.
Trigger > Action.
Triggers fall into three categories: Time, Event, and Visual Cue (examples below).
Actions are preplanned habits to be executed immediately and without hesitation or thinking, once a Trigger is identified.
After a Trigger is identified in the real world, you are unauthorized to negotiate with yourself or take time to decide what to do because your preplanned Action was chosen by you to help you achieve your goals and live your values.
The only authorized time to negotiate with yourself over what to do after a Trigger is observed is during preparation and planning.
The "Trigger > Action" Technique may also be referred to as Task Bracketing and is also similar to David Goggins' "One Second Decision."
  • Time Trigger:
    At 9 pm (Trigger) > Take melatonin to sleep better (Action)
  • Event Trigger:
    After Breakfast (Trigger) > Brush Teeth (Action)
  • Visual Cue Trigger:
    Don't forget my keys. Put keys in a specific spot on the counter in the kitchen. Walk by keys later (Trigger) > Pick up keys (Action)

Not sure where to start? Write about the Situation for five minutes.
The Situation can reveal anything about any step in the problem solving process.
  • The Situation > Write about what's going on, gather your thoughts and feelings about The Situation (feelings reveal what's important to you)
  • The Situation > What's going wrong? What's going right? How do you feel about it?
  • The Situation > Brainstorm ideas
  • The Situation > Identify a Value/Fear, Goal, or Problem to focus on. Go there.

Authorities and Leaders drive the entire problem solving process.
Authorities and Leaders can reveal the Situation, Values, Fears, Goals, and Problems.
  • Authority/Leader > Correctly identify, analyze, and understand the Authority/Leader
  • Authority > Write about them
  • Authority/Leader > Brainstorm who they might be
  • Authority/Leader > Prioritize the key decision maker(s)
  • Authority/Leader > What is the Situation?
  • Authority/Leader > Reveals Values and Fears, go there

Values and Fears -- or Standards and Worries -- are what Authorities and Leaders care about most in the world.
Only emotions and actions can reveal someone's true Values and Fears. Therefore, emotions and actions are foundational aspects of -- and crucial to -- any problem solving process.
When someone makes a decision based on deep emotions and feelings, they often describe it as following their gut or heart. I think technically it's localized to the brain's limbic system, but I'm not a neuroscientist.
Values elicit positive emotions while Fears elicit negative emotions.
Values and Fears can reveal Goals.
Values and Fears are two sides of the same coin.
Identify a Value (Strength, Wisdom) and you immediately know the associated Fear (Weakness, Stupidity), and vice versa.
Identify a Value or Fear, ask, "Why this Value/Fear?" Keep asking and answering for five times and you'll typically arrive at a truly Core Value or Fear.
Probably the most important Values and Fears are the ones you're hiding or avoiding because they contain the greatest insights into your true character.
  • Value and Fear > Write about it, study it, understand it, solve it
  • Value and Fear > Brainstorm Values and Fears
  • Value and Fear > For each idea, ask, "Why this Value/Fear?" Keep asking and answering for five times, which is applying the Five Whys structured analytic technique to find deeper and more important Core Values and Fears
  • Value and Fear > Prioritize Values and Fears that matter most
  • Value and Fear > Values/Fears reveal Goals and Problems > Go there

Goals help us live our Values and avoid our Fears.
Goals are also Solutions to Problems.
Goals can reveal their Solutions and Problems.
There are two types of Goals: 1) Live/Achieve a Value, 2) Avoid a Fear.
Goals to avoid Fears are 100-200% more effective at driving behavior than Goals to achieve Values.
Try combining them: Get in better shape and Avoid being fat.
Can you feel the difference? It sure isn't fun to hear but we can either take the less painful way toward our Goals, or the more effective way.
Progress over perfection.
Not making progress toward at least one of our Goals or failing to live our Values significantly contributes to feelings of depression and anxiety.
We innately need to feel progress toward our Goals or our body will start to fire the alarm bells to try to get us back on track to living our Values.
It's the journey that matters most, not the destination.
  • Goals > In service of which value or avoidance of which fear?
  • Goals > Who are these Goals for? What Authority or Leader?
  • Goals > What are the Goals?
    1) Brainstorm a list of Goals. Write them down, study them, and understand them.
    2) Get organized by creating lists, categories, groups, and chunks.
  • Goals > Which Goals are the most important?
    Prioritize the list of Goals.
  • Goals > Plan and Schedule. Create To-Do Lists, set Reminders and Deadlines. Move on and relax!
  • Goals > Not sure how to answer any of these questions?
    Ask for help. Search for answers.
  • Goal > Who is this Goal for?
    The Authority or Leader.
  • Goal > What is the Goal?
    Create the Goal's' Name. Suggested format: "Achieve something and Avoid something."
  • Goal > Write about it, study it, and understand it
  • Goal > Why is this Goal important?
    What Value and Fear does this Goal serve? Not sure yet? Ask "Why is this Goal important?" and answer it five times to find the Value and Fear this Goal serves. The last answer is the Value and Fear.
  • Goal > How do you achieve this Goal? You need Solutions and Actions. Go to Solutions.
  • Goal > Plan and Schedule. Create To-Do Lists, set Reminders and Deadlines. Move on and relax!
  • Goal > Not sure how to answer any of these questions? Ask for help. Search for answers.
  • Goal > Encounter a Problem or Obstacle? Go to Problems.
  • Goal > Having trouble trying to achieve the Goal? Feel like giving up? The Goal is probably too big or takes too long to achieve, which can lead to giving up way too early. Try breaking down the Goal into smaller ones or shorter time frame Goals to tackle and conquer. Build momentum and countless incremental gains, progress, and victories. Progress over perfection.

Problems are obstacles in the way of Goals and Solutions.
Problems reveal their Solutions.
  • Problem > Correctly identify, analyze, and understand the Problem
  • Problem > Write about it
  • Problem > Brainstorm ideas
  • Problem > For each idea, ask, "Why this Problem?" Keep asking and answering for five times to find deeper and more important Problems
  • Problem > Prioritize Problems that matter most
  • Problem > Identify future Problems to solve now and prepare for later
  • Problem > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Problem > Stuck? Break it down into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Problem > Reveals Goals, go there
  • Problem > Reveals Solutions, go there

Solutions resolve Problems and achieve Goals.
Solutions need Tested in the Real World to get crucial Feedback.
  • Solution > Correctly identify, analyze, and understand the Solution
  • Solution > Write about it
  • Solution > Brainstorm ideas
  • Solution > For each idea, ask, "Why this Problem?" Keep asking and answering for five times to find deeper and more important Solutions
  • Solution > The quickest way to the best Solution/Answer is to share the Wrong Solution/Answer with a large group of people, i.e. Cunningham's Law, and watch people clamor at the opportunity to prove you wrong (and give you the best Solution/Answer as a result)
  • Solution > Prioritize Solutions that matter most
  • Solution > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Solution > Stuck? Break it down into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Solution > Needs Tested in the Real World to get crucial Feedback, go there

Real World Tests and Experiments provide critical feedback for Ideas and Solutions.
Real World Tests and Feedback need to be Reviewed.
  • Real World Test > Get real-world feedback, ground truth, evolution by natural selection
  • Real World Test > Immersion or solitude, mass data collection
  • Real World Test > After Action Review, Performance Review

After Action Reviews and Performance Reviews reveal insights of Real World Tests and Experiments.
Insights gained from Reviews need to be applied and implemented through an Improvement Plan.
  • After Action Review > What went well, what didn't go well
  • After Action Review > Lessons learned
  • After Action Review > What gets measured, gets improved
  • After Action Review > Make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • After Action Review > Improvement Plans, Adapt, Overcome

Improvement Plans apply the insights of Reviews.
The Improvement Plan reveals what Adapted Actions to Practice and Gain Experience in.
  • Improvement Plan > Make an improvement plan, make changes, do more of what you did right, less of what you did wrong
  • Improvement Plan > Iterate, progress over perfection
  • Improvement Plan > Adapted Actions, Practice, Gain Experience

Adapted Actions carry out the Improvement Plan.
Practice these Actions and Gain Experience to get better incrementally, achieve Goals, and live Values.
  • Adapted Actions > Spaced repetition, consistently, gain experience
  • Adapted Actions > Get your reps in, you shouldn't have to think eventually, just act intuitively
