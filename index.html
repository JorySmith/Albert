Getting Started

What many people don't do, but nearly all of the top performers consistently do
  • Find the Light in the Darkness. Running away from problems is the greatest problem in the world. Solutions are found within the Problem they solve. Try becoming antifragile by learning, growing, and thriving from your problems and fears. Try to visit but not remain in the Darkness (Problem) to find the answers (Solutions) you seek. Especially the scariest Problems that undoubtedly contain the Answers that will make you stronger, wiser, and more resilient. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Aim for the Highest Good. Try avoiding the consequences of purposeless nihilism, which is pure hell. Nobody's perfect, but try taking responsibility for your life and try to be a better person. Having a good Aim literally changes your reality and vision. An Aim reveals what you actually see in the world. An Aim reveals the righteous path you've chosen and the Problems and Obstacles in your way. If you're not growing, you're dying. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
  • Seek the Truth. Embrace it. Trusting in lies is a one way ticket to misery and hell. Try trusting only the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, and watch it lead towards inner peace and greatness. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Write it all Down. Talk about it. You will not believe how much of your voice is locked away in a prison, handcuffed, and silenced. That is until you try this. Thoughts, worries, frustrations, fears, problems, goals, etc. Anything and everything you can think of. Writing supercharges clarity and problem solving skills. It also frees your soul and liberates the value you can bring to the world. Also, consider sharing it, teaching it, and sharpening it. 1 2 3 4
  • Chip away at Problems and Goals. Stuck? The Goal, Problem, or Solution is probably too big or takes too long, which can lead to giving up way too early. Try breaking them down into smaller ones or shorter time frames to tackle and conquer. Build momentum and countless incremental gains, progress, and victories. Progress over perfection. You can always make improvements later as you iterate. 1 2 3 4 5 6
  • Ask for Help. Call for Aid. You're Not Alone. You don't have to struggle alone. Two minds are better than one. Also, most Problems have already been solved. You need only search for their Solutions. 1 2 3 4
  • Develop a Process. Create Habits. Look for Triggers around you to spark Action. Wisdom is meaningless and powerless without consistent Actions and Habits. A Process helps automate and Trigger those Actions and Habits that apply all of the knowledge and skills you've acquired. Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action. Trigger > Action. And if you've done your preparation, you'll know exactly what Action to take for maximum gain. 1 2 3 4
  • Organize and Structure your Mind. A chaotic and disorganized mind is a chaotic and disorganized life. Try creating ordered lists, categories, groups, and chunks to make information easier to understand, remember, and convert into wisdom. 1 2 3 4
  • Plan and Schedule. Try not to invite suffering into your life by losing track of the critical things you need to do to achieve your goals and live your values. Try creating to-do lists and setting reminders and deadlines. Deadlines help you avoid overworking and analysis paralysis. 1 2 3 4
  • Brainstorm Ideas. No idea is bad here. Try to express your deepest Curiosity and Creativity. Quantity over quality. Leave judgement at your door. Try writing down as many ideas, possibilities, options, and alternatives as possible. 1 2 3 4
  • Prioritize what Matters. Try not to waste your time on things that don't matter. Some things are far more important and impactful than other things. Quality over quantity. Think 80/20 Principle, which is derived from the Pareto Principle. 1 2 3 4
  • Take Breaks for Breakthroughs. Stubbornly fighting a problem despite seeing very little progress or results isn't good for anyone. You won't believe how fast solutions pop up if you take a break. Try activating divergent thinking in your mind. Do something else, think about something else, go for a walk, or leave and come back. 1 2 3 4
  • Provide Demonstrations and Examples over Explanations. Explanations are the least effective form of communication. From debates to teaching, examples and demonstrations provide far more detail, usefulness, and persuasiveness. 1 2 3 4
  • Ask Someone to do Something over Bossing Them Around. If you want to move slower, undermine outcomes, and degrade relationships, then bossing people around is a great strategy. Little freedom, low productivity. Try involving others, collaborating, and enjoying the power of team membership and buy-ins. 1 2 3 4 5
  • Focus on what You can Control. If you can't control it, you're wasting your time on it. Try moving on to what you can control and where you can make a difference. 1 2 3 4
  • Test Ideas and Solutions. Ideas not accountable to real world results are near worthless at best and harmful at worst. The path to hell is paved with good intentions. An untested idea can be the most beautiful fraud in the world. Try getting feedback from other people to fill in your blind spots. What gets measured, gets improved. 1 2 3 4 5
The Problem Solving Process

The "Trigger > Action" Technique

Like an alarm clock, Triggers are clear reminders in the world to take Action.
Trigger > Action.
Triggers fall into three categories: Time, Event, and Visual Cue.
Triggers may also be referred to as "Task Bracketing."
  • Time Trigger:
    At 9 pm (Trigger) > Take melatonin to sleep better (Action)
  • Event Trigger:
    After Breakfast (Trigger) > Brush Teeth (Action)
  • Visual Cue Trigger:
    Don't forget my keys. Put keys in a specific spot on the counter in the kitchen. Walk by keys later (Trigger) > Pick up keys (Action)

The Authority drives the entire problem solving process.
The Authority reveals The Situation, Fears, Values, Goals, and Problems.
  • Authority > Write about them, brainstorm, develop them fully, correctly ID the Authority, fully understand them
  • Authority > Why? 5 Why's to dig deeper
  • Authority > Fears
  • Authority > The Situation
  • Authority > Fears/Dangers/Monsters
  • Authority > Values/Beliefs/Purpose

Not sure where to start? Write about The Situation.
The Situation can reveal Fears, Values, Goals, and Problems.
  • The Situation > Write about it, gather thoughts and info. What's wrong? What's going right? How do you feel?
  • The Situation > Identify a Goal or Problem to focus on > Go to Goals or Problems

Fear according to which Authority?
Fears can reveal Problems and Goals.
Fears can also reveal Values (Fears and Values are two sides of the same coin).
  • Fear > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the value, fully understand it
  • Fear > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Values
  • Fear > To what Authority/Audience? > Go to Authority/Audience
  • Fear > Refine, reduce data, prioritize the most impactful
  • Fear > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Fear > Problems/Obstacles
  • Fear > Goals/Tasks/Dreams

Value according to which Authority?
Values can reveal Goals and Problems.
Values can also reveal Fears (Values and Fears are two sides of the same coin).
  • Value > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the value, fully understand it
  • Value > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Values
  • Value > Also a Solution to what Problem? > Redo Problems for it
  • Value > To what Authority/Audience? > Go to Authority/Audience
  • Value > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Value > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Value > Goals/Tasks/Dreams

There are two types of Goals: 1) Achieve some Value, 2) Avoid some Fear.
Goals to Avoid Fears are at least two to three times more effective at driving behavior.
Goal to avoid which Fear?
Goal in service of which Value?
Goals can reveal Solutions and Problems.
  • Goal > Write about it, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the goal, fully understand it
  • Goal > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Goals
  • Goal > Also a Problem > Go to Problems/Obstacles
  • Goal > Goal/Task to what Value?
  • Goal > Stuck? Break down Goals into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Goal > Identify Future Ones to solve and prepare for now
  • Goals > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Goal > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Goal > Solutions
  • Goal > Problems

Problem for which Goal or Solution?
Problems can reveal Solutions.
  • Problem > Write about it, obstacles/restrictions, brainstorm, develop it fully, correctly ID the problem (are there deeper Problems/Obstacles?), fully understand it
  • Problem > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Problems/Obstacles > Redo Problems/Obstacles for each
  • Problem > Stuck? Break down Problems into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Problem > Identify Future Problems/Obstacles to solve now so you can prepare
  • Problem > Also a Solution to which Deeper Problem? > Redo Problems/Obstacles for it
  • Problem > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Problem > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Problem > Goals
  • Problem > Solutions

Solution to which Problem or Goal?
Solutions need Tested in the Real World to get crucial Feedback.
  • Solution > Write about it, brainstorm, pros and cons, cost-benefit analysis, develop it fully, correctly ID the Solution, fully understand it
  • Solution > Why? 5 Why's to Deeper Problems
  • Solution > Stuck? Break down Solutions into smaller ones or shorter time frames and chip away at each one, make incremental progress, tackle easy low hanging fruit, build momentum
  • Solution > Come with Problems, Consequences? Cons? > Go to Problems/Obstacles
  • Solution > To what clearly wrong Solution that causes outrage? Cunningham's law
  • Solution > Solution to what Problem/Goal/Task? > Go there to develop further
  • Solution > Refine, reduce data, prioritize
  • Solution > Create a To-Do List, Plan, Schedule, Deadline, move on and relax
  • Solution > Needs Tested and Real World Feedback

Real World Test for which Solution?
Real World Tests and Feedback need to be Reviewed.
  • Real World Test > Get real-world feedback, ground truth, evolution by natural selection
  • Real World Test > Break into Smaller Ones, make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Real World Test > Review of Test Results/Performance

Review for which Real World Test/Feedback?
Reviews need to be Implemented through an Improvement Plan.
  • Review > What went well, what didn't go well, what you problems did you avoid, what goals did you achieve
  • Review > What gets measured, gets improved
  • Review > Make incremental progress, chip away at it
  • Review > Improvement Plans/Adapt/Overcome

Improvement Plan based on which Review?
The Improvement Plan reveals what Adapted Actions to Practice and Gain Experience in.
  • Improvement Plan > Make an improvement plan, make changes, do more of what you did right, less of what you did wrong
  • Improvement Plan > Iterate, progress over perfection
  • Improvement Plan > Actions/Practice/Gain Experience

Do these Actions according to which Improvement Plan?
Practice these Actions and Gain Experience to get better incrementally, achieve your Goals, and live your Values.
  • Actions > Spaced repetition, get experience
  • Actions > Get your reps in, you shouldn't have to think eventually, just act intuitively
